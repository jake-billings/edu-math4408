Course Objectives

  • We will begin by acquiring knowledge of the basic tools in Graph Theory (models and representations, basic results on degrees, isomorphism, and connectivity. Then we will move on to more advanced topics such as Trees, Planarity, and Graph Coloring (including the Four Color Theorem), thickness, and games with graphs. If time permits, we will talk about the graph algorithm that is at the heart of Google's success.

Contractible edge (one that preserves connectivity). Existence of contractible edges in simple 3-connected graphs. First half (and harder part) of Kuratowski's Theorem: a graph with no subdivision of K5 or K3,3 is planar.
